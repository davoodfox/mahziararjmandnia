Hashish

Technical Narrative

The generation of this image began with a Python script that defined the initial conditions of cellular automata. Layered with stochastic noise, the simulation evolved through Galapagos Optimization, mimicking organic growth patterns. The subtle distortions added by image processing resembled the fractal intricacy of hashish leaves. Culebra facilitated the data flow in Grasshopper, ensuring spatial coherence.

Poetic Prose

Through the veil of calculation, patterns emerged like leaves suspended in smoke. Cellular automata whispered secrets to Galapagos, their symphony echoing the chaos of nature. A dance of data, fragmented yet whole, crystallized into the mosaic—an ode to the fleeting geometry of hashish.

Philosophical Reflection

Hashish, as both concept and construct, symbolizes the interplay of chaos and order. Through genetic algorithms and cellular simulations, we observe how complexity arises from simple rules, a metaphor for creativity itself. In the computational void, art finds its seed.

Historical Contextualization

The intricate pattern of this image draws inspiration from the ornamental geometry of Middle Eastern hashish paraphernalia. By simulating cellular automata within Python, we revive ancient aesthetics through modern algorithms, creating a bridge between the analog past and the digital future.

Curatorial Project

tehranXperiment

TehranXperiment was a dynamic event series that advanced exploration in the arts, which I had the privilege of curating. Showcasing experimental, collaborative, and alternative expressions, the platform played a pivotal role in propelling the thriving alternative art scene in Tehran. Dedicated to nurturing the growth of Iranian alternative art, it provided a supportive space for both emerging and established artists. TehranXperiment transcended traditional art performances and exhibitions by featuring interactive elements such as audio-visual installations and interdisciplinary performances, engaging audiences with diverse forms of artistic expression. The event took place on Saturday, 13 January 2024. Building on its success, the project extended to Helsinki under the name HelsinkiXperiment, with the invaluable support of my friend Pajud, who resides in Finland. This expansion brought the experimental and collaborative spirit of the series to an international audience, fostering a global dialogue in alternative arts.

Intractive Stage Design

I designed the stage for this theater production with a strong emphasis not only on static design but also on creating an immersive and interactive experience. By utilizing video mapping technology in TouchDesigner, I developed a dynamic visual system that responded to every movement of the actors and actresses on stage. The mapping was programmed to interact with each step they took, seamlessly integrating their performances with the environment. This innovative approach transformed the stage into a living, responsive space, enhancing the audience’s engagement and blurring the boundaries between performance and design.
